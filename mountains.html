The mountains of the Adirondacks are known for their difficulty and rewarding experience when hiking. While there are over one hundred mountains to climb in the region, only 46 of them are of enough difficukty and reputation to be taken on by hikers. People who have climbed all of these 46 high peaks are part of the club called "The 46ers".

Algonquin and Mt. Marcy are the only two peaks in the Adirondacks that are higher than 5,000 feet. Both of these mountains take a whole day to climb round trip. Mount Marcy is also the highest peak in all of New York.
